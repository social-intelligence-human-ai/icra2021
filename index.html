Social Intelligence in Humans and Robots

Workshop ICRA 2021 - May 31, June 4

About
Social intelligence is at the core of both human and artificial intelligence. From a young age, humans can understand, interact, collaborate, and communicate with each other. Most of what we learn is taught by others, or learned in a social context. Thus, a truly intelligent AI agent should be able to understand and work with humans as well as other AI agents.

This workshop focuses on the challenges and developments in building AI systems equipped with social intelligence, and leverages theories and insights from studies of human social intelligence for achieving such goals. In particular, the workshop will explore what it would take for machines to:

  1. Understand the behaviors and mental states of humans
  2. Engage in rich and complex interactions with humans
The workshop will bring together experts from cognitive science and developmental psychology to better understand the principles and origins of human social intelligence, and experts from AI and Robotics, to discuss how to engineer socially intelligent artificial agents, and how these paradigms can be deployed in both virtual and real scenarios.

The workshop will focus on 2 main themes:


Human Social Intelligence

Cognitive scientists and developmental psychologists have been studying the cognitive mechanism and acquisition of human social intelligence for decades. We believe that insights from these fields, detailing how social intelligence grows in human minds, could be critical for engineering social intelligence in machines. Therefore we include a panel of cognitive scientists and developmental psychologists, who study and build computational models of social cognition, and can speak to how social intelligence starts, and how it grows, in human minds.

This theme will include topics such as: Social Perception and Theory of Mind, Human Cooperation, Competition, and Communication


Robot Social Intelligence

We will explore the main challenges in building systems that can understand human goals, intentions and their beliefs to effectively collaborate with them. We will invite AI researchers and roboticists to bring their perspective into what technologies are necessary to enable such capabilities, and discuss challenges and methods to bring these advances into robotics, enabling human-AI collaboration into the real world. We plan to focus on the following topics:

This theme will include topics such as: Robot Social Perception and Theory of Mind, Human-AI Collaboration and Social Learning, Human-Robot Interaction

Areas of interest
The primary focus areas of this workshop are the following: We will accept submissions focusing on Human and Robot Social Intelligence. The primary areas of interest will be the following:

  1. Social Perception and Theory of Mind: How humans acquire theory of mind capabilities. What computational models approximate these capabilities in humans?
  2. Human Cooperation Competition and Communication: Origins and development human communication or competition; Inference of these relationships
  3. Machine Theory of Mind: How to infer what an agent or robot knows about an environment, how to make robots learn about the minds of other robots or humans
  4. Human-Robot Interaction: Methods to understand human intentions or offer assistance; social perception, communication between humans and robots; learning to convey information for better collaboration; probing and gathering information for collaboration.
  5. Interdisciplinary Efforts: We welcome works that integrate efforts in cognitive sciences and robotics, to advance social intelligence between humans and robots.
